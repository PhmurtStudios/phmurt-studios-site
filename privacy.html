1. Information We Collect

We collect information in various ways to provide, maintain, and improve our Service. The types of information we collect include:

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your email address, password (stored as a cryptographic hash, never in plaintext), and display name.
  • Character Data: When you create and manage D&D characters in our application, we collect character attributes, class information, stats, inventory, and related game data.
  • Campaign Data: Information about campaigns you create or participate in, including campaign settings, notes, session logs, and participant information.
  • User-Generated Content: Any content you create within the Service, including character descriptions, campaign notes, and gameplay records.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Analytics Data: We collect information about how you use the Service, including page visits, features accessed, time spent on pages, click patterns, and user interactions. This data helps us understand how users engage with our application and improve the user experience.
  • Device Information: We automatically collect certain information about your device, including browser type, operating system, device type, and unique device identifiers.
  • Log Data: Our servers automatically log information such as your IP address (for security and analytics purposes), access times, pages viewed, and referring URLs.
  • Cookies and Similar Tracking Technologies: We use cookies and similar technologies to track user behavior and preferences (detailed in Section 4).

1.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third-party services if you choose to connect your account with external platforms or if you participate in features that involve third-party integrations.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes:

  • Service Provision: To create and maintain your account, authenticate your identity, and provide the core functionality of the Service.
  • Personalization: To customize your experience, remember your preferences, and deliver personalized content and features.
  • Improvement and Development: To understand how you use the Service, identify areas for improvement, and develop new features and functionality.
  • Analytics: To analyze usage patterns and trends to optimize the Service's performance and user experience.
  • Security and Fraud Prevention: To monitor, investigate, and prevent fraudulent activity, unauthorized access, and other security threats.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes.
  • Communication: To send you service-related announcements, security alerts, and other important notifications.

3. Data Storage and Security

3.1 Where Your Data Is Stored

  • Supabase Database: Your account information, character data, and campaign data are stored in Supabase, a hosted PostgreSQL database service. Supabase maintains servers with appropriate security measures to protect data integrity and confidentiality.
  • Browser Storage: We use browser localStorage and cookies to store certain client-side data, such as authentication tokens, theme preferences, and session information. This data is stored on your local device.
  • Third-Party CDNs: Static assets such as fonts and libraries are served through jsDelivr and Google Fonts CDNs.

3.2 Security Measures

We implement multiple layers of security to protect your information:

  • Row-Level Security (RLS): Our database uses RLS policies to ensure users can only access their own data and data shared with them explicitly.
  • Content Security Policy (CSP): We implement CSP headers to mitigate injection attacks and control resource loading.
  • Encrypted Connections: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS protocols.
  • Password Hashing: Passwords are securely hashed and salted before storage, never stored in plaintext.
  • Authentication Tokens: Session tokens are used for authentication and are subject to expiration policies.

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use the following cookies and similar tracking technologies:

4.1 Authentication Cookies

  • phmurt_sb_auth: Supabase authentication session cookie. Stores your session token to keep you logged in across page visits. Expires after a period of inactivity.
  • phmurt_sess: Additional session cookie for managing user state and authentication status within our application.

4.2 Preference Cookies

  • phmurt_theme: Stores your theme preference (light/dark mode) so your visual preference persists across sessions.

4.3 Analytics

We may use analytics cookies and similar technologies to track usage patterns and understand how visitors interact with our Service. This information helps us improve the user experience.

Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. Instructions for doing so are typically found in your browser settings. However, refusing cookies may limit your ability to use certain features of the Service.
