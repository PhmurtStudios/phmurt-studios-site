Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Overview

This Cookie Policy explains what cookies and similar browser-storage technologies (such as localStorage and sessionStorage) are used on phmurtstudios.com (the "Service"), why we use them, how long they last, and how you can control them. This policy supplements our Privacy Policy.

We only use cookies and storage items that are strictly necessary to run the site or to remember preferences you have chosen yourself. We do not use third-party advertising cookies, cross-site tracking cookies, or data brokers.

1. What are cookies and browser storage?

Cookies are small text files that a website places in your browser so the site can recognize you on later visits. Browser storage APIs (localStorage, sessionStorage) are similar: they let a site store small amounts of data in the browser itself. Both are restricted to the site that set them — other sites cannot read them.

We use a mix of cookies and browser storage to keep you signed in, remember your preferences, and run the application.

2. Cookies and storage we set

2.1 Strictly necessary (authentication & session)

These are required for the site to function. They are set when you log in and are removed when you log out. Blocking them will prevent you from using account features.

  • phmurt_sb_auth — Supabase authentication cookie. Stores the token that keeps you signed in across page loads. Expires when your session expires (or sooner if you are inactive). First-party; HttpOnly where applicable.
  • phmurt_sess — In-memory/session marker used to coordinate tab-level session state. Cleared when you close the browser or log out.

2.2 Preferences

These remember small choices you have made. They are not used for advertising.

  • phmurt_theme — Stores your chosen theme (for example, light or dark). Used only to render the site the way you asked. Persists until you clear browser storage or change the setting.

2.3 First-party analytics

We log a small amount of usage data to our own database (Supabase) so we can understand how the site is being used and fix bugs. This happens server-side — we do not share it with third-party analytics providers.

  • phmurt-sid (sessionStorage) — A random session identifier used only to correlate page views and feature events within a single visit. It is not a login token and is not tied to your identity on its own. It is cleared when you close the tab.

The corresponding server-side records (page visits, feature events, and client errors) include your account ID only while you are logged in. If you delete your account, those records are anonymized (account ID is set to null), consistent with our Privacy Policy.

2.4 Third-party cookies

We do not intentionally set any third-party advertising or cross-site tracking cookies. However, the following third parties may set their own cookies or log requests when their resources are loaded on our pages:

  • Stripe — when you visit a billing or checkout page. Used for fraud prevention and to complete payments. Stripe's cookie policy is available at stripe.com/cookie-settings.
  • Google Fonts — may log font requests. Google Fonts' privacy statement is at developers.google.com/fonts/faq/privacy.
  • jsDelivr CDN — may log requests for JavaScript libraries. See jsdelivr.com/privacy.

We do not control these services' cookies and are not responsible for their content.

3. How to control cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. The exact steps vary by browser but are usually found under Settings → Privacy or Preferences → Cookies. Some browsers also let you block cookies on a per-site basis.

You can also clear your browser's localStorage and sessionStorage for phmurtstudios.com from the same menu. This will sign you out and reset your saved preferences.

Please note: if you block strictly necessary cookies, you will not be able to sign in or use account-gated features.

4. Do Not Track

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. There is no industry agreement about how sites should respond to DNT, and we do not currently change behavior based on it. Because we do not use cross-site tracking or advertising cookies, the DNT signal does not meaningfully apply to us.

5. Changes to this policy

We may update this Cookie Policy when we add features, change vendors, or in response to legal changes. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Material changes may also be highlighted on the site.

6. Contact

Questions about this Cookie Policy? Reach out at contact@phmurtstudios.com. To exercise your data rights (access, export, deletion), see our self-service page at account-privacy.html.
